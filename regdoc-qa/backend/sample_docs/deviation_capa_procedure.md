# SOP-DEV-022: Deviation and CAPA Management

> SAMPLE / SYNTHETIC document created for portfolio demonstration only. Not a real controlled procedure.

**Document No.:** SOP-DEV-022  **Version:** 4.2  **Effective Date:** 20-Jan-2026

## 1. Purpose
To describe the process for reporting, investigating, and resolving deviations from approved
procedures and specifications, and for managing the resulting Corrective and Preventive Actions
(CAPA).

## 2. Deviation Classification
2.1 **Critical** — a deviation that may affect patient safety, product efficacy, or data integrity.

2.2 **Major** — a deviation that may affect product quality but is unlikely to affect patient safety.

2.3 **Minor** — a deviation with no foreseeable impact on product quality.

## 3. Timelines
3.1 A deviation shall be reported to Quality Assurance within 1 working day of detection.

3.2 Critical deviations shall be investigated and closed within 30 calendar days. Major deviations
shall be closed within 45 calendar days. Minor deviations shall be closed within 60 calendar days.

3.3 Any extension to an investigation timeline requires documented justification approved by the
Head of Quality.

## 4. Investigation
4.1 Root cause analysis shall be performed using a recognised methodology such as the 5-Whys or
Ishikawa (fishbone) diagram.

4.2 The investigation shall determine product impact, identify the root cause, and define
corrective and preventive actions.

## 5. CAPA
5.1 Corrective actions address the immediate cause; preventive actions prevent recurrence across
similar processes or equipment.

5.2 Each CAPA is assigned an owner and a target completion date.

5.3 An effectiveness check shall be performed no earlier than 90 days after CAPA implementation to
confirm that the action resolved the issue without introducing new problems.

## 6. Responsibilities
6.1 The originating department raises the deviation and supports the investigation.

6.2 Quality Assurance assigns classification, oversees the investigation, and approves closure.

6.3 The Head of Quality approves timeline extensions and reviews critical deviations.
