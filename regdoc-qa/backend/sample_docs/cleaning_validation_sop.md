# SOP-CLN-014: Cleaning Validation of Product-Contact Equipment

> SAMPLE / SYNTHETIC document created for portfolio demonstration only. Not a real controlled procedure.

**Document No.:** SOP-CLN-014  **Version:** 3.0  **Effective Date:** 12-Jan-2026

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the requirements for validating cleaning processes of product-contact
equipment in oral solid dosage manufacturing to ensure that residues of the previous product,
cleaning agents, and microbial contaminants are reduced to acceptable levels.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all shared product-contact equipment in Manufacturing Blocks A and B, including
fluid-bed dryers, blenders, tablet presses, and coating pans.

## 3. Acceptance Criteria
3.1 Chemical residue shall not exceed the Maximum Allowable Carryover (MACO) calculated using
the 10 ppm criterion and the 1/1000th of minimum therapeutic dose criterion; the most
stringent of the two governs.

3.2 Swab recovery studies must demonstrate a recovery of not less than 70% for the analyte
of interest before swab results are considered valid.

3.3 Total Organic Carbon (TOC) of the final rinse water shall not exceed 500 ppb.

3.4 Microbial bioburden of rinse samples shall not exceed 100 CFU per mL, with absence of
objectionable organisms.

## 4. Sampling
4.1 Both swab sampling and rinse sampling shall be used. Swab sampling targets the hardest-to-clean
locations identified in the equipment risk assessment.

4.2 A minimum of three consecutive successful cleaning runs is required to establish validation.

## 5. Responsibilities
5.1 The Production Officer performs cleaning per the equipment cleaning SOP and records activities.

5.2 Quality Control performs swab and rinse sample analysis and reports results.

5.3 Quality Assurance reviews the validation data and approves the cleaning validation report.

## 6. Re-validation
Cleaning processes shall be re-validated whenever a new product is introduced, equipment is
modified, or the cleaning agent is changed. A periodic review of cleaning validation status is
performed every 24 months.
