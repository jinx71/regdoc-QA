# SOP-CAL-007: Calibration of Measuring and Test Equipment

> SAMPLE / SYNTHETIC document created for portfolio demonstration only. Not a real controlled procedure.

**Document No.:** SOP-CAL-007  **Version:** 5.1  **Effective Date:** 03-Feb-2026

## 1. Purpose
To define the program for calibration of measuring and test equipment used in GMP operations so
that measurements remain traceable to national standards and fit for their intended use.

## 2. Calibration Intervals
2.1 Analytical balances shall be calibrated monthly and verified daily before use with certified
reference weights.

2.2 Temperature mapping probes and data loggers shall be calibrated every 6 months.

2.3 Pressure gauges on critical utilities shall be calibrated every 12 months.

2.4 pH meters shall be calibrated daily prior to use using a minimum of two buffer solutions
that bracket the expected measurement range.

## 3. Tolerance and Acceptance
3.1 An instrument is acceptable when the deviation between the instrument reading and the
reference standard is within the manufacturer's stated tolerance, or ±2% of the reading where no
manufacturer tolerance exists.

3.2 Each calibrated instrument shall bear a calibration label showing the calibration date, the
next due date, and the identity of the person who performed the calibration.

## 4. Out-of-Tolerance (OOT) Handling
4.1 When an instrument is found out of tolerance, it shall be immediately labelled "OUT OF
SERVICE" and removed from use.

4.2 An impact assessment shall be performed to evaluate the effect on product released since the
last successful calibration. The assessment is initiated within 1 working day and documented as a
deviation per SOP-DEV-022.

## 5. Responsibilities
5.1 The Engineering Department maintains the calibration schedule and performs or coordinates
calibrations.

5.2 Quality Assurance approves the calibration master list and reviews OOT impact assessments.

5.3 Equipment users verify that an instrument is within its calibration validity period before use.

## 6. Records
Calibration certificates shall be retained for a minimum of 5 years and made available during
regulatory inspections.
